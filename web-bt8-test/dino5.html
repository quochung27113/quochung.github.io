Iguanodon Dinosaurs

Iguanodon Fast Facts

  • You pronounce their name 'ig-wah-na-don'.
  • The meaning of its name is 'iguana tooth'.
  • They lived in the early Cretaceous Period.
  • It was a herbivore.
  • Their length was up to 12 meters (39 feet)
  • They weighed 3.5 tons (about as much as a van).

More Dinosaur Details

These dinosaurs were herbivores. Scientists are not exactly sure what they ate, but they probably grazed these Cretaceous plants.

They lived 126-125 million years ago in the Early Cretaceous Period.

Iguanon had a really long little finger which it used to gather food. Their thumbs had spikes on top to help them stab food. And their teeth would be replaced throughout their lives to help them chew tough plants. They may also have used their thumb spike to protect themselves against predators. Its dinosaur classification is Ornithopoda.

Although Ornithopod dinosaurs are usually bird like dinosaurs that walk on two feet - these dinosaurs walked around on all fours as their arms were just 25% shorter than their legs.

Where did they live?

Most Iguanodons lived in Europe (including England, Belgium and the Isle of White) but fossils have also been found in Africa and North America.

How big are they?

They were big and bulky - growing to about 12 meters (39 feet) long. Their weight meant they wouldn't have been fast. It is thought they could get up to about 14 miles per hour.
